Generative AI Cover Maker – Full Project

Description & Motivation

The Generative AI Cover Maker is a creative tool designed to automatically generate album-style playlist covers based on mood, theme, or genre prompts. People love customizing playlists, yet most default to generic covers because designing custom art takes time or graphic design skill.

We wanted to make playlist expression easy, fun, and personal — allowing users to translate music taste into visual art in seconds. With AI image generation becoming more accessible, this project explores how users interact with creative AI tools and how interface design impacts trust, creativity, and usability.

Online Study

Our initial online study helped us understand user expectations for playlist art. Participants viewed different covers and shared what styles felt meaningful versus random. We also asked how they perceive AI-generated visuals and what customization control they would want.

Results showed a strong preference for covers that visually reflect the playlist theme. Many users wanted the ability to tweak mood, color, and style — confirming that AI should generate quickly but still leave room for personal creative input.

Prototyping Phase

We created two low-fidelity prototypes to compare interaction flow:

  • Design A – Step-by-Step Guided Flow: Connect → Generate → Customize → Download
  • Design B – Dashboard Layout: All tools visible on one page at once

The goal was to evaluate which interface supported creativity, clarity, and confidence. Participants used printed and digital mockups and completed core tasks like connecting a playlist, generating an AI cover, and customizing it.

User Studies Results

User Study 1 – Low-Fidelity Prototype Comparison

Users tested both prototypes and rated usability, clarity, and creativity. All three participants completed tasks faster with Design A and expressed more confidence in a structured guided flow.

Key Findings:

  • Design A was easier to navigate and more intuitive.
  • The dashboard layout caused confusion due to unclear labels and starting points.
  • Participants described Design A as “smooth,” “organized,” and easier to follow.

Decision: We moved forward with the Step-by-Step guided flow.

Planned Improvements: Add undo/redo, presets, and clearer customization UI.

User Study 2 – Functional Prototype Test

Study 2 evaluated the working app on a laptop with real interaction. Users performed the full flow and we recorded confusion points and usability strengths.

What Worked Well:

  • Playlist connection was consistently smooth and fast.
  • Users enjoyed watching the AI generate covers that matched their playlist.
  • Downloading the final cover was clear and straightforward.

Challenges Observed:

  • Most confusion occurred during the customization step.
  • Participants sometimes mis-clicked buttons or expected more styling options.
  • Some UI elements looked draggable or interactive when they were not.

Iteration Plan:

  • Clarify the customization page layout and navigation.
  • Add labels, hints, and/or tooltips for each control.
  • Expand style presets to support more visual variety.

Conclusion: The main flow (Connect → Generate → Customize → Download) is intuitive and enjoyable. Future work will focus on improving customization clarity and expanding creative control.
